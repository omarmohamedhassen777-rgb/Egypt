My favorite food in Egypt

Kushari

Koshary, kushari or koshari (Egyptian Arabic: كشرى [koshari]) is Egypt's national dish and a widely popular street food.[1] It is a traditional Egyptian staple, mixing pasta, Egyptian fried rice, vermicelli and brown lentils,[2][3] and topped with chickpeas, a garlicky tomato sauce, garlic vinegar, and crispy fried onions. Sprinklings of garlic vinegar and hot sauce are optional.

fool w taameya

Ful and taameya are two of the most popular traditional Egyptian foods. Ful is made from slow-cooked fava beans and is usually eaten for breakfast. It is healthy, filling, and full of flavor, especially when mixed with olive oil, lemon, garlic, and spices. Taameya, which is the Egyptian version of falafel, is made from ground fava beans mixed with herbs and spices, then fried until crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Together, ful and taameya are a classic combo that represents Egyptian street food culture and is loved by people from all walks of life.
